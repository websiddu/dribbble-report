Dribbble Team

How could we change the design of the Dribbble teams page to give teams their own identity within the product and to separate it from the individual page design?

Requirement

Here is what requested by the customers

  • Better way to brand their teams
  • Should be different from normal user profiles
  • Highlight team remembers
  • Also, highlight on who worked on which project

Research

  • We know that 60% of posts are work done on behalf of a team or company, but are posted to an individuals account.
  • Based on user research, we have observed that other users are unclear who actually worked on a specific post within a teams page.
  • 40% of teams have not uploaded their avatar, and thus we do not expect them to customize their page beyond the default.
  • Based on user research, we have observed that users donâ€™t understand if the team they are looking at is a large team with many members, or a small team with few members.

Discovery

Making informed decision is a key for creating a successful design solution. Below, I have written my thought process and ideas on how I have tried to approach this problem with a simple yet effective solution.

Constraints & Assumptions

Though it seems a short design exercise there were many factors to consider and requires a deep understand of the product. For this particular exercise, I made some reasonable assumptions based on the constraints. Here are some of them,

  • Design should blend in with the current dribbble design. Team page should not feel like its disconnected from other pages.
  • Should follow branding guidelines.
  • Should be responsive.
  • Highlight team members.
  • Minimum/No effort is expected from the teams.
  • Are teams interested in stats on their team pages? (Unknown)
  • Time is a constraint (3 - 5 hours).

Ideation

There were multiple issues to address based on the research and initial website evaluation. I addressed each of them below

  1. Team:
    In current design the team members are displayed as a list of images and only 15 members of the team. And, I'm not sure how these users are picked. And the total number of users are listed on the top menu.
  2. Team shots vs Personal Shots:
    In current design, it's kind of tedious to find out who is responsible for the design.
  3. Branding:
    Currently, there is not way that a team can brand themselves. This can be done using multiple options.
    1. Using cover pictures.
    2. Changing the link colors to brand colors.
  4. Projects:
    Projects are created by teams and each project can contain multiple related shots. Currently there is less stress on this. Even popular teams don't post projects.
